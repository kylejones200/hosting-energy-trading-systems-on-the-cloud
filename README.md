## Business context

Energy trading doesn't look like it used to. The market has moved far beyond traders on phones shouting across noisy floors. It now runs on algorithms, predictive models, and cloud-native software platforms. And that shift is accelerating.

In the last five years, trading EBIT for oil, agriculture, metals, LNG, and power and gas has nearly doubled. What's behind that growth? Digital advantage through faster and smarter data --- used in real time, at scale, and across portfolios. The next wave of competitiveness won't come from cheaper barrels or better hedging strategies. It'll come from software. From systems that can ingest weather data, predict imbalances, rebalance positions, and book trades automatically.

European power markets are leading the way. Structural changes are pulling in small-scale renewables, creating volatility, and increasing the volume of intraday and daily trades. Imbalance trades are spiking. Dispatch is fragmenting. And every trader, from small IPPs to national utilities, now needs advanced trading platforms to keep up. That means ETRM systems --- energy trading and risk management software --- are becoming core infrastructure.
